# Cannabis Terpene Selection Guide

## How to Choose Strains Based on Desired Effects

Rather than selecting cannabis strains by THC percentage or name recognition, experienced consumers and growers select based on **terpene profiles**. Terpenes are the aromatic compounds that determine how a specific strain will make you feel.

## Quick Reference: Terpene → Effect

| Terpene | Primary Effect | Best For | Aroma |
|---------|---------------|----------|-------|
| Myrcene | Sedation/Relaxation | Sleep, pain | Earthy, musky |
| Limonene | Mood elevation | Anxiety, depression | Citrus |
| Caryophyllene | Anti-inflammatory | Pain without sedation | Peppery |
| Linalool | Calming | Insomnia, anxiety | Lavender |
| Pinene | Mental clarity | Daytime focus | Pine |
| Humulene | Appetite suppression | Weight management | Hoppy |
| Terpinolene | Creativity | Artistic work | Herbal |

## Understanding Terpene Interactions

Terpenes work together in what researchers call the **entourage effect**. A strain's total experience comes from the combination of all terpenes present, not just the dominant one.

Common synergistic combinations:
- **Myrcene + Caryophyllene**: Enhanced pain relief
- **Limonene + Linalool**: Mood elevation with calming
- **Pinene + Limonene**: Clear-headed energy

## How Growing Environment Affects Terpene Expression

The same genetic clone can produce different terpene profiles depending on growing conditions:

- **UV exposure**: Higher UV-B increases terpene production
- **Temperature**: Lower night temps (60-65°F) enhance terpene preservation
- **Harvest timing**: Earlier harvest = more monoterpenes (pinene, limonene). Later harvest = more sesquiterpenes (caryophyllene, humulene)
- **Drying conditions**: Slow dry (60°F, 60% RH, 10-14 days) preserves volatile terpenes
