# Getting Started with Cannabis Clones — Complete Beginner Guide

## First-Time Clone Grower? Start Here.

This guide covers everything from choosing your first clones to harvesting your first crop.

## Step 1: Choose Your Genetics

Before you buy clones, decide:

### What's your growing environment?
- **Indoor (controlled):** Any strain works. Choose based on desired effect and yield.
- **Outdoor (California):** Choose strains that finish before fall rain (early-mid October in NorCal).
- **Greenhouse/Light Dep:** Fast-finishing strains (8 weeks or less) work best.

### What's your experience level?
- **Beginner:** Choose forgiving, vigorous strains. Avoid finicky exotics until you have basics down.
- **Intermediate:** Try higher-quality genetics that need more attention.
- **Advanced:** Run exotic or sensitive genetics that reward precise environments.

- Veg: 75-80°F, 55-65% RH, 18 hours light

## Step 3: Receive and Acclimate Your Clones

When clones arrive:
1. Inspect for pests and damage
2. Place in humidity dome (closed) under gentle light (200-300 PPFD)
3. Keep dome closed for 3 days
4. Days 4-7: Gradually open dome vents
5. Day 7-10: Remove dome completely
6. Day 10-14: Transplant into final containers

## Step 4: Vegetative Growth (4-8 weeks)

After transplanting:
- Light: 18 hours on / 6 hours off
- Feed: 1/4 strength first week, ramp to full by week 3
- Water: When top 1-2 inches of soil is dry (lift pots to judge weight)
- pH: 6.0-6.8 for soil, 5.5-6.2 for coco
- Training: Top once after 5-6 nodes. LST (bend and tie) to create even canopy.
- Length: 4-8 weeks depending on desired final plant size

## Step 5: Flip to Flower

When plants fill 70-80% of your canopy:
1. Switch light to 12/12 (12 hours light, 12 hours COMPLETE darkness)
2. Zero light leaks during dark period (tape pinholes, seal zippers)
3. Switch to bloom nutrients
4. Plants will stretch 50-100% in height first 2 weeks

## Step 6: Flowering (8-10 weeks for most strains)

Week-by-week expectations:
- Weeks 1-2: Stretch, first pistils appear
- Weeks 3-5: Buds forming and fattening
- Weeks 6-8: Maturation, trichomes developing
- Weeks 8-10: Harvest window (check trichomes with 60x loupe)

## Step 7: Harvest, Dry, Cure

### Harvesting
- Cut whole plant or individual branches
- Remove large fan leaves

### Drying (10-14 days)
- Hang in dark room: 60-65°F, 55-62% RH
- Good air circulation (gentle fan on wall, not on buds)
- Ready when small stems snap (not bend)

### Curing (2-8 weeks)
- Trim buds, place in glass jars
- Fill jars 75% full
- First week: Open jars 2x daily for 10 min ("burping")
- After week 1: Open 1x daily
- After week 2: Open every 2-3 days
- Quality improves for up to 8 weeks of curing

## Common Beginner Mistakes

1. **Overwatering** — #1 killer. Water when soil is DRY, not on a schedule.
2. **No pH testing** — causes 90% of "deficiency" problems.
3. **Light leaks in flower** — causes hermaphrodites. Check EVERY pinhole.
4. **Adding too many nutrients** — less is more. Start at half strength.
5. **Harvesting too early** — patience! Wait for trichomes to be 10-20% amber.
6. **Drying too fast** — rushed drying = harsh smoke. Low and slow (10-14 days).
