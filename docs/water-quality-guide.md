# Water Quality Guide for Cannabis Growing

## Understanding Your Water Source

Water quality is the invisible foundation of cannabis nutrition. Bad water causes more mysterious plant problems than any other single factor.

## Key Water Parameters

### What to Test

| Parameter | Ideal Range | Impact |
|-----------|-------------|--------|
| pH | 6.0-7.0 (source) | Nutrient availability |
| EC/PPM | < 200 PPM | Baseline nutrient load |
| Chlorine | 0 PPM | Kills beneficial microbes |
| Chloramine | 0 PPM | Persists longer than chlorine |
| Calcium | 40-100 PPM | Cell wall structure |
| Magnesium | 20-50 PPM | Chlorophyll production |
| Sodium | < 50 PPM | Root toxicity at high levels |
| Iron | < 5 PPM | Stains, potential lockout |
| Bicarbonates | < 200 PPM | pH buffering (raises pH) |
| Sulfates | < 100 PPM | Nutrient component |

### Source Water Categories

**City/Municipal Water (PPM 50-300)**
- Contains chlorine or chloramine
- Consistent but may have high bicarbonates
- Chlorine off-gasses in 24 hours (leave in open container)
- Chloramine does NOT off-gas — requires carbon filter or treatment

**Well Water (PPM varies widely)**
- May contain high minerals (Ca, Mg, Fe, Mn)
- Often alkaline (pH 7.5-8.5)
- Can fluctuate seasonally
- Iron staining is common at >5 PPM

**RO (Reverse Osmosis) Water (PPM 0-20)**
- Near-pure water, complete control over mineral content
- Must add Cal-Mag back (RO removes everything)
- Best for hydro and coco operations
- Equipment cost: $200-2000 depending on capacity

**Rain Water (PPM 5-30)**
- Nearly pure but may contain atmospheric pollutants
- pH typically 5.5-6.5 (slightly acidic)
- Excellent for organics if collected cleanly
- Storage and contamination risk (algae, mosquitoes)

## pH Management

### Why pH Matters

Nutrients become unavailable outside the correct pH range regardless of how much you add:
- Iron locks out above pH 6.5
- Phosphorus locks out below pH 5.5 and above 7.0
- Calcium locks out below pH 6.0
- Most nutrients available at pH 6.0-6.5 (soil) or 5.8-6.2 (hydro/coco)

### pH Adjustment
- **pH Down:** Phosphoric acid (common), citric acid (organic), sulfuric acid (industrial)
- **pH Up:** Potassium hydroxide, potassium silicate (adds Si benefit)
- Always add nutrients FIRST, then adjust pH last
- pH drift is normal in reservoirs — check and adjust daily

## Water Temperature

| Application | Ideal Temp | Risk |
|------------|-----------|------|
| Irrigation | 65-72°F | Below 60°F shocks roots, above 75°F = low O2 |
| Reservoir (hydro) | 65-68°F | Above 72°F = root rot (Pythium) |
| Foliar spray | 68-72°F | Cold spray can shock stomata closed |
| Clone domes | 72-78°F | Root zone warmth speeds rooting |

## Watering Practices by Medium

### Living Soil
- Water when top 1-2 inches are dry
- Water thoroughly until 10-20% runoff
- Frequency: every 2-4 days depending on pot size and plant stage
- Never let soil go completely bone dry (kills microbes)

### Coco Coir
- Feed multiple times daily in flower (3-6 fertigation events)
- Always water to 15-20% runoff
- Never let coco dry out completely (creates hydrophobic spots)
- EC of runoff should be within 20-30% of input EC

### Hydroponics
- Maintain reservoir at target EC and pH
- Change reservoir completely every 7-14 days
- Top off with half-strength solution as water level drops
- Monitor dissolved oxygen (air stones essential)

## Genetics and Water Interaction

Different genetics have different water demands:
- Heavy drinkers need more frequent irrigation
- Compact indicas typically use less water than stretchy sativas
- HLVd-infected plants show abnormal water uptake patterns

Starting with verified clean genetics ensures predictable water consumption patterns and nutrient uptake.
