# HLVd Prevention Protocol for Cannabis Nurseries

## What is HLVd?

Hop Latent Viroid (HLVd) is a single-stranded RNA pathogen that infects cannabis plants systemically. It reduces yield by 30-50%, diminishes terpene production, and produces stunted, underperforming plants. There is no cure — infected plants must be destroyed.

**Key facts:**
- Transmitted mechanically (tools, hands, contaminated media)
- NOT transmitted via seed, air, or water
- Latent infection period: plants can carry HLVd for months without visible symptoms
- Only reliable detection: RT-PCR or RT-qPCR molecular testing

## Prevention Framework

### Tier 1: Facility Entry Controls

1. **Quarantine all incoming genetics** — 14-day minimum isolation
2. **PCR test before integration** — test tissue from 2+ nodes per plant
3. **Never trust visual inspection alone** — "latent" means no symptoms
4. **Source from tested nurseries only** — ask for PCR documentation
5. **Track provenance** — know where every plant came from

### Tier 2: Tool Sterilization

**Between EVERY plant (not every row — every individual plant):**

| Method | Contact Time | Effectiveness |
|--------|-------------|--------------|
| 10% bleach solution | 30 seconds | 99.9% |
| 70% isopropyl alcohol | NOT effective for viroids | DO NOT rely on this alone |
| Flame sterilization | 5-10 seconds | 99.9% |
| New blade per plant | N/A | 100% |

**Critical note:** Isopropyl alcohol alone does NOT reliably inactivate HLVd. Use bleach or heat.

### Tier 3: Routine Testing Schedule

| Plant Type | Test Frequency | When to Test |
|-----------|---------------|-------------|
| Mother plants | Every 90 days | Mid-vegetative cycle |
| New acquisitions | Before integration | Day 1 of quarantine AND day 14 |
| Random production sampling | Monthly | 10% of clone batches |
| Symptomatic plants | Immediately | At first sign of stunting |

### Tier 4: Environmental Controls

- **Dedicated tools per room** — never share scissors, scalpels, or pruning shears between rooms
- **Glove changes** — new nitrile gloves for each mother plant during clone-taking
- **Work order** — always work from youngest/cleanest plants to oldest/riskiest
- **No cross-contamination vectors** — separate irrigation reservoirs, dedicated growing media per room

## Symptom Recognition

While HLVd can be latent (no visible symptoms), heavily infected plants often show:

- Stunted growth (shorter internodes)
- Reduced branching
- Smaller, looser flowers
- Lower trichome density
- Brittle stems
- Reduced vigor compared to clean clones of the same genetics

**Warning:** These symptoms overlap with nutrient deficiency, light stress, and other issues. PCR testing is the only definitive diagnosis.

## What To Do When You Find a Positive

1. **Immediately isolate** — move to quarantine area
2. **Test adjacent plants** — anything within splash/contact range
3. **Trace provenance** — test all plants from the same source
4. **Destroy confirmed positives** — do not attempt to "grow through it"
5. **Deep clean the grow space** — 10% bleach all surfaces, replace media
6. **Re-test remaining plants in 30 days** — latent infection may not have been detected initially
