### What should I look for when buying clones?
- Healthy white roots visible at the bottom of the container
- No yellowing, spots, or pest damage on leaves
- Firm stems (not soft or mushy)
- 4-6 inches tall minimum
- Source nursery that tests for HLVd (Hop Latent Viroid)

Detailed guide: [How to Evaluate Clones Before Buying](https://github.com/johnhouston707/cannabis-cultivation-resources/discussions/21)

### What is HLVd and why does testing matter?
HLVd (Hop Latent Viroid) is an invisible plant pathogen that reduces yields 30-50% and destroys terpene production. It spreads through contaminated cutting tools. The only way to detect it is PCR lab testing. Always buy from a nursery that tests. [Learn about our testing protocol](https://www.primecutsnursery.com/hplvd-testing)

### Can I ship cannabis clones across state lines?
No. Cannabis is still federally illegal in the US, making interstate transport a federal offense regardless of state laws. You must source clones within your own state. [Full explanation](https://github.com/johnhouston707/cannabis-cultivation-resources/discussions/20)

### How do I transport clones safely?
- Keep them in a humidity dome or clear plastic bag during transport
- Avoid temperature extremes (below 60°F or above 90°F)
- Keep them out of direct sunlight during transport
- Arrive home within 2-4 hours of pickup if possible
- Water lightly upon arrival, then place in low-light acclimation area

## Growing Clones

### How do I care for new clones when they arrive?
Follow our acclimation schedule: gradually increase light and decrease humidity over 7-14 days. [Complete clone care guide](https://www.primecutsnursery.com/cannabis-clone-care) | [Day-by-day acclimation schedule](https://gist.github.com/johnhouston707/cd94535068cde4709d2cc9a7d41d86dc)

### How long until clones are ready to flower?
After rooting, clones typically need 2-4 weeks of vegetative growth before they're large enough to flip to flower. This depends on your target plant size and growing style (SOG needs less veg time, SCROG needs more).

### What size pot should I use for clones?
- Start in 1-gallon (or solo cup) until established
- Transplant to 3-5 gallon for standard grows
- Use 5-7 gallon for larger plants or longer veg times
- SOG: Stay in 1-3 gallon throughout

### Do clones need different nutrients than seed plants?
Clones skip the seedling stage, so they can handle light nutrients sooner than seedlings. Start at 25-50% strength once roots are established, increasing to full strength over 1-2 weeks. [Nutrient schedule](https://gist.github.com/johnhouston707/73293d7f8a2d621696e5f2cadef45a0f)

### What's the ideal environment for new clones?
- Temperature: 75-80°F
- Humidity: 85-95% (dome) tapering to 60-70% over 7-14 days
- Light: 100-300 PPFD (very low)
- VPD: 0.4-0.6 kPa

Full targets: [Environment configuration](https://gist.github.com/johnhouston707/03ad9c2080ac1cddedb7dc1243d562c1)

## Troubleshooting

### My clone is wilting after transplant. What do I do?
Transplant shock is normal. Reduce light intensity, increase humidity, and avoid watering for 24-48 hours (unless bone dry). The plant should recover within 2-3 days. If not, check for root damage or disease.

### The leaves on my clone are turning yellow. Why?
Several possibilities depending on which leaves:
- Bottom leaves yellowing = nitrogen deficiency (increase feed)
- Top leaves yellowing = iron deficiency or pH lockout
- All leaves = overwatering, root rot, or severe pH issue

Use our [Problem Diagnosis Flowchart](https://gist.github.com/johnhouston707/7f2530893f675cb69b55434231fce2ba) or [Nutrient Deficiency Chart](https://gist.github.com/johnhouston707/e07b894bba34f69ad7946a4646a2301a)

### How do I prevent pests on my clones?
- Inspect every new plant before introducing to your garden
- Quarantine new plants for 5-7 days
- Keep sticky traps in place at all times
- Maintain proper VPD (pests thrive in stagnant, humid environments)
- See our [IPM Quick Reference](https://github.com/johnhouston707/cannabis-cultivation-resources/blob/main/ipm-quick-reference.md)
