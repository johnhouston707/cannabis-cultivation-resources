# Cannabis IPM Quick Reference

## Top 5 Nursery Pests

| Pest | Detection | Damage | Treatment |
|------|-----------|--------|-----------|
| Broad Mites | 60x loupe required | Glossy twisted new growth | Forbid → Avid → Suffoil-X rotation |
| Russet Mites | 60x loupe, bronze leaves | Progressive yellowing, brittleness | Same as broad mites + sulfur burner |
| Fungus Gnats | Visible adults, sticky traps | Root damage to clones | Mosquito Bits (Bti), dry media |
| Thrips | Silver streaks, blue sticky traps | Leaf scarring, virus transmission | Spinosad, Amblyseius cucumeris |
| Powdery Mildew | White spots on leaves | Systemic, reduces photosynthesis | Potassium bicarbonate, airflow |

## Prevention Calendar

**Weekly:**
- Inspect new growth under 60x loupe
- Check sticky traps (yellow for gnats, blue for thrips)
- Monitor humidity levels (keep below 60% in flower)

**Monthly:**
- Preventive Suffoil-X application on mothers
- Release beneficial insects (Swirskii or Cucumeris)
- Deep clean propagation area between batches

**Every New Introduction:**
- 14-day quarantine minimum
- Preventive dip in Suffoil-X before entering production
- Visual inspection under magnification

## Biocontrol Agents

| Beneficial | Controls | Release Rate | Notes |
|-----------|----------|-------------|-------|
| Amblyseius swirskii | Broad mites, thrips | 50-100/m² | Preventive only |
| Amblyseius cucumeris | Thrips larvae | 100/m² | Works in cool temps |
| Stratiolaelaps scimitus | Fungus gnat larvae | 25,000/500 sq ft | Soil-dwelling |
| Steinernema feltiae | Fungus gnat larvae | Drench application | Fast-acting |

## Spray Schedule (Vegetative/Mothers Only)

Rotate products to prevent resistance:

**Week 1:** Suffoil-X (horticultural oil)
**Week 2:** Spinosad (thrips + caterpillars)
**Week 3:** Potassium bicarbonate (PM prevention)
**Week 4:** Beauveria bassiana (broad-spectrum fungal)
**Repeat**

Never spray in flower — plan your IPM before flip.

## Emergency Response

**If you find broad/russet mites:**
1. Treat ENTIRE room, not just affected plants
2. 3 applications, 3 days apart (covers lifecycle)
3. Quarantine room — no material in or out for 14 days
4. Re-inspect at day 14 before all-clear

**If you find powdery mildew:**
1. Remove all visibly infected tissue
2. Increase air circulation immediately
3. Apply potassium bicarbonate (contact kill)
4. Mark affected mother — do NOT take clones from her

## The HLVd Connection

Piercing-sucking pests (mites, thrips) create mechanical wounds that are theoretical HLVd transmission vectors between adjacent plants. Clean IPM = reduced viroid risk.
