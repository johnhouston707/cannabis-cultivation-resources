# Cannabis Phenotype Selection Guide

## What is Phenotype Selection?

When growing cannabis from seed, each seed expresses genetics differently — even siblings from the same cross. Phenotype selection (\"pheno hunting\") is the process of growing multiple seeds and choosing the best expression to keep as a clone mother.

## Why It Matters

The difference between a mediocre and exceptional cut of the same strain comes down to phenotype selection. The Wedding Cake that wins cups is not random — it is a specific phenotype selected from dozens or hundreds of seeds, chosen for its exceptional expression of desirable traits.

## The Selection Process

### Phase 1: Germination & Veg (Weeks 1-4)

Observe and eliminate:
- [ ] Remove any males immediately (if regular seeds)
- [ ] Remove runts with poor vigor
- [ ] Remove any with abnormal growth patterns
- [ ] Note: branching structure, internode spacing, leaf shape

Keep detailed labels. Photograph everything.

### Phase 2: Flower (Weeks 1-4 of 12/12)

Observe structure:
- [ ] Stretch ratio (how much vertical growth after flip)
- [ ] Branching pattern and cola distribution
- [ ] How it responds to training (if applicable)
- [ ] Nutrient sensitivity or resilience
- [ ] Speed of flower formation

### Phase 3: Flower (Weeks 5-harvest)

Evaluate quality:
- [ ] Trichome density (use 60x loupe)
- [ ] Trichome head size (bigger = more resin)
- [ ] Bud structure (dense vs airy)
- [ ] Color expression (purples, pinks)
- [ ] Smell intensity and profile
- [ ] Resistance to mold/pests
- [ ] Finishing speed

### Phase 4: Post-Harvest

Final evaluation:
- [ ] Dry weight yield per plant
- [ ] Trim time (easy vs difficult bud structure)
- [ ] Cure quality (how terps develop over 2-4 weeks)
- [ ] Smoke/vape test (effects, duration, onset speed)
- [ ] Potency testing (if available)
- [ ] Stability over multiple test sessions

## Scoring Matrix

Rate each phenotype 1-10 on these categories (adjust weights based on your goals):

| Category | Weight (Commercial) | Weight (Personal) |
|----------|-------------------|------------------|
| Yield | 25% | 10% |
| Bag Appeal | 20% | 15% |
| Terpene Profile | 15% | 30% |
| Potency | 10% | 25% |
| Grow Difficulty | 15% | 5% |
| Finish Time | 15% | 15% |

## Common Selection Mistakes

1. **Selecting too early** — Never select solely based on veg performance. The best vegging plant often isn't the best flower producer.

2. **Selecting on yield alone** — High yield with mediocre quality loses in premium markets.

3. **Not running enough seeds** — Minimum 10 seeds per cross to find something special. 20-50 is better for serious selection.

4. **Ignoring stability** — A pheno must perform consistently across multiple runs before committing as a mother.

5. **Keeping too many** — Each mother plant requires space, nutrition, and testing. Be ruthless in elimination.

## The HLVd Checkpoint

Before committing to any phenotype as a permanent mother:
- PCR test for HLVd
- Quarantine for 14 days
- Retest before integration into mother room

A perfect phenotype is worthless if it carries HLVd. Every clone taken from it will be infected.

## After Selection

Once you have identified exceptional phenotypes:
1. Take multiple backup clones immediately
2. Establish as mother plant in dedicated veg space
3. Document everything (photos, grow notes, test results)
4. PCR test on 90-day schedule going forward
5. Share the genetics — good cuts deserve to be grown
